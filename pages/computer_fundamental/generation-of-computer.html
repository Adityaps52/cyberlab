Generation of computer

The Generation of computer refers to the different phases of development in computer technology. There are 5 generation and each generation is characterized by a significant advancement in its structure or architecture, size, hardware and its processing capabilities.

There are five generations of computer known till date.

  1. First Generation (1940 - 1956)
  2. Second Generation (1956 - 1963)
  3. Third Generation (1964 - 1971)
  4. Fourth Generation (1971 - Till present)
  5. Fifth Generation (Present and beyound)

First Generation (1940 - 1956)

The first computers used vacuum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory. This computer were very large in size,expensive, and generated a significant amount of heat. During the first generation computer there was no programming language, So they were programed using machine language,assembly language and their calculation were relatively slow compared to modern computer stands.

  • Advantages
    1. ENIAC and UNIVAC laid the foundation stone for future advancement in computer technology.
    2. Complex calculation and computation made possible. Primarly used in scientific and military purpose.
    3. : It reduced the time and effort required for complex mathematical tasks.
  • Disadvantages
    1. Very Large in size as they consist of thousands of vacuum tubes, wires, and other components so ,their size made them impractical for personal use.
    2. High Power Consumption and Heat Generation:Vacuum tubes were consumed a significant amount of power and generated a substantial amount of heat these issues led to frequent over heating .
    3. Limited Memory and Processing Speed: The memory capacity was limited , and their processing speed was relatively slow compared to modern standards. They were unable to handle large amounts of data efficiently
    4. No UI / UX : Interaction with the computer was through punched cards or input/output devices, and requiring specialized training.
    5. High Cost: : Cost is high and used mainly by government agencies and research institution.

Second Generation(1956 - 1963)

  • The second generation computer used Transistor as its main electronic component. It replaced the Vaccum tube which resulted in reduction in size, faster computation, consumed less power, less heat generated and many more.
  • Magnetic core memory is used for core storage.it is more faster than punched card and magnetic drums
  • CPU(Central Processing Unit) ,memory ,programming language and input and output units came in the second generation.
  • Programming language like FORTAN, ALGOL and COBOL were used for programming.
  • Some of the examples of second generation commputer are PDP-8, IDM1400 serise,IBM7090 and 7094, UNIVAC1107,CDC3600 etc
  • Advantages
    1. Second Generation computer were Faster in comparision to first generation computer.
    2. Smaller in size as compared to the first generation.
    3. Smaller in size as compared to the first generation.
    4. Transistors consumed less power and were more reliable than vaccum tubes.
    5. Reduction in dimension lead to better portability.
  • Disadvantages
    1. It still relied on Magnetic Core Memory.
    2. Still there is a need of Cooling system as heat is still a major issue in this generation.
    3. Transistors were expensive and more costly, so second generation computer are not cost effective.

Third Generation (1964 - 1971)

  • In Third generation, technology shifted from transistor to Integrated Circuit which allowed multiple transistors to be placed on a single chip( silicon chip)
  • A variety of transistors were placed on silicon chips ,called semiconductors.
  • Third generation computer also used semiconductor memory and operating system for multitasking
  • Some of the examples of third generation computer are IBM System 360 and DEC PDP - 10
  • Advantages
    1. Smaller in size as compared to previous generation
    2. Processing speed of this computers are enhance and overall performance are boosted as compared to previous
    3. High Storage Capacity as compare to previous generations
    4. Less heat production as compare to other generations of computer
    5. Introcudtion of Operating System enabled multitasking and timesharing
    6. Input devices like mouse and keyboard were used instead of punched card
  • Disadvantages
    1. Size were relatively large and heavy compared to modern devices
    2. Air conditioning was required as heat is still produced by third generation.

Fourth Generation (1971 - Till present)

  • The fourth generation occurs from 1971 to present time. In this generation, Microprocessor was used for first time
  • The electronic component used was Large Scale Integration(LSI)
  • Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) later replaced LSI circuite for smaller size with large capacity.
  • Techonology like Time Sharing, Multiprocessing, Operating speed and Virtual Memory made it more user friendly.
    • Advantages
      1. Smaller in size and more reliable then other generation of computer.
      2. The heat generation was negligible.
      3. Less cost and less repair time .
      4. The most advantage of this technology is the one microprocessor can contain all the circuits required to perform arithmetic ,logic, and control function on one chip .
    • Disadvantages
      1. In this generation it is hard to storing and processing large amount of data.
      2. Very advanced technology was required to fabric the ics .
      3. Fourth Generation computers faced security risks.

    Fifth Generation (Present - Beyond)

    • The technology behing the fifth generation of computer is Artificial Intelligence (AI) .
    • Various new technology came into existance like voice commands, facial recognition and many more.
    • Various new technology came into existance like voice commands, facial recognition and many more.
    • The fifth generation computer are the fastest computer as compared to other computer.
    • The size of this computer are smallest , produces less heat, consumes less electricity and process the data at highest speed.
